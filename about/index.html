My story

From the classroom to the cobblestones

Before I guided full time, I worked in a school. Years spent with children of every age taught me something I use on every tour: history sticks when it's told as a story, not a list of dates. It's why families with young children feel just as at home on my walks as history-loving couples and solo travellers - I adapt the storytelling to whoever is standing in front of me.

Every tour I lead is fully private. That means the pace is yours: time for questions, for photos, for a coffee, and for the spontaneous detours that so often turn out to be the best part of the day. I'd rather you leave with a feeling for the city than a head full of facts you'll forget by dinner.

Some of my tours turn to a more recent, more personal chapter. I was a child in Šibenik during the Croatian Homeland War of the early 1990s. On those walks I share not only the historical events - like the September 1991 Battle of Šibenik - but my own childhood memories, and the stories of family and neighbours who lived through it. I tell them with care, and with respect for what they mean to this city.

Šibenik isn't just where I work - it's home. Let me show you why.
